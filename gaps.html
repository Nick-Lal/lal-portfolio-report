The honest ledger

Every gap, and what closes it

A verification project is only as trustworthy as its list of unknowns. This page is that list — the questions public records cannot answer, the properties that resisted verification, and the specific, concrete steps that would close each gap.

The four questions no public record can answer

1 · Who owns the LLC — and in what split?
ATL Ventures LLC has never filed a report naming an owner. Closes it: the operating agreement and the entity tax returns (K-1s).
2 · Which houses are occupied, and at what actual rent?
Court dockets prove the rental operation but hide unit addresses. Model rent estimates exist for only 51 parcels. Closes it: the leases and rent roll — or municipal rental-license lists via Right-to-Know requests to the ten relevant municipalities.
3 · What is each property's cash flow and maintenance cushion?
Taxes are being paid (verified), so cash flows; how much and to where is private. Closes it: bank statements and the business books.
4 · What is the manager's effective fee?
No management agreement is recorded anywhere. Closes it: the agreement, if one exists, or the books.

The estate is the master key. The founder's estate — never opened, per the recorder's index — likely controls both the 22 deceased-titled parcels and his interests in the entities. Opening it (or confirming with the Register of Wills why not) unblocks almost everything above.

Next steps, in order

    Ten-minute records calls

    Register of Wills (estate + date of death); PA Treasury unclaimed-property lookup. Both remote, both free.

  2. 2

    Request the four private document sets

    Estate file · entity returns/K-1s · leases/rent roll · bank statements or management agreement. Framed by the September 30 LLC deadline — these answer questions the manager needs answered for his own protection too.

  3. 3

    Counsel before action

    A Pennsylvania estates & partnership attorney to interpret the estate freeze and unregistered-name titles. This site organizes records; it is not legal advice.

    Open the estate, bring the LLC filings current, regularize the unregistered titles — then the family can decide the portfolio's future (professional management, partial sale, or hold) from clean paper.

About this site. Every fact here comes from a public government record — county assessment and deed data, the PA Department of State, county Tax Claim lookups, and the PA court portal — captured with a saved evidence artifact (268 entries). Market values and rents are model estimates, not appraisals. Data as of 2026-07-07; ownership is re-verified against the county weekly. Where a fact could not be verified it is marked Unverified rather than guessed.

Privacy. Court information appears only as aggregates: no tenant is named anywhere on this site, by design. Owners and deed parties are named exactly as they appear in public county records.
