Methodology

Built to be checked, not believed

This project's standard was simple: a confident blank beats a plausible guess. Everything below describes how each class of fact was established, what would falsify it, and where the edges of public knowledge sit.

Source hierarchy

When sources conflict, the higher one wins:

  1. County assessment & Recorder of Deeds records: ownership, value, deeds, sales
  2. PA Department of State / PENN File: entity existence, status, officers
  3. County Tax Claim system: real-estate tax delinquency
  4. Redfin / Zillow: market value and rent estimates only, never ownership
  5. The 2018 list and blogs: leads only, never evidence

Evidence discipline

Every fact has a receipt
268 captured artifacts (screenshots, CSV exports, PDFs), each tied to the specific fact it supports and its source URL, indexed in the evidence register. Any number on this site can be traced to its capture.
Methods were validated with positive controls
A “no delinquency found” answer is only meaningful if the search works. Before recording a single clean tax result, the same lookup was run against a control parcel with a known outstanding balance ($16,340.64 due) to prove the system returns hits when hits exist. Ambiguous screens were re-run, never recorded.
Unfindable stays unfindable
Eleven of the 99 listed addresses match no parcel in any county database. They are carried as Unverified, with the failed searches documented on each dossier, rather than force-matched to a plausible parcel.
Names were matched cautiously
County spellings are inconsistent (AMRIT/ARMIT, RST/RSP, NAGPAUL/MAGPAUL/NAGPAUI). One-letter variants were treated as match candidates, noted explicitly, and capped at medium confidence rather than silently merged.
Nominal deeds were not counted as sales
Recorded prices of $0, $1 or $10 are family/entity housekeeping transfers, not market events; they are labeled as such wherever they appear.
How research statuses map to this site
The internal register classifies each 2018-list row as Confirmed Lal, Not Lal, or Uncertain. Here those render as Verified family-held, Sold / third party, and Unverified; the “Uncertain” rows are exactly the eleven addresses with no verifiable county record.
The work is monitored, not frozen
The county parcel layer is re-pulled weekly and diffed; any title change on any of the 127 tracked parcels is flagged automatically. Findings carry their as-of date (2026-07-07).

The court record, and why no tenant is named here

The PA court portal yielded 170 dockets involving the family names and entities: 157 landlord-tenant filings spanning 2011-2026 (the remaining 13 are civil-collection and municipal code-enforcement matters involving the same names, surfaced by the same sweep), with $751,637 in judgments for the family as plaintiff and stated rents from $500 to $3,000 per month (average about $942). A possession order in the most recent case was served 2026-07-06. In 2025 and 2026, municipal code-enforcement waves appear: about 36 rental-license citations in Valley Township alone, plus Coatesville citations including a guilty verdict against “Maple Ventures”.

Two hard limits shape what this can tell you. First, public dockets identify tenants by name and town but hide unit addresses, so they prove an active rental operation without revealing which specific houses are rented. Second, tenants named in eviction cases are private individuals in a difficult moment; this site deliberately publishes aggregates only, never tenant names.

What the estimates are, and what they are not

Redfin and Zillow model estimates cover 58 of the 110 family parcels, totaling ≈$14,745,776; 51 of those 58 also carry a rent model, totaling ≈$103,829/mo. They are statistical models, not appraisals; they skip most vacant land and many non-MLS addresses entirely. County assessed values cover all 110 but reflect assessment ratios, not market prices. For the 58 parcels where both figures exist, market estimates run about 4.2× assessed. No such comparison exists for the other 52 parcels, so no “whole-portfolio market value” is stated anywhere on this site. A professional valuation would start from these numbers, not end at them.

Derived estimates and field checks for the unpriced 52

Fifty-two family parcels have no Redfin or Zillow model estimate. Rather than leave them blank, each carries a clearly labeled derived range built from two cited anchors:

  1. Low anchor: assessed value × 3.27, Pennsylvania's official Common Level Ratio factor for Chester County (PA Dept. of Revenue, 2025 factors ↗, applicable July 2026 to June 2027). The state uses this exact multiplier to convert assessed values to market value for transfer-tax purposes. Within this portfolio it is genuinely conservative: it sits at the 17th percentile of the ratios observed on the 58 parcels where both figures exist.
  2. Mid anchor: assessed value × 4.26, the median market-to-assessed ratio of those same 58 verified pairs. Applied only where a structure is confirmed on the parcel, by county land-use class or by a July 2026 aerial-imagery check; vacant land gets the low anchor only, because ratios derived from houses say little about a lot.

Each unpriced parcel also got a field pass in July 2026: aerial imagery (Esri World Imagery) classified as structure or vacant land, and a web search for listings, ads, or news. Where a real listing or model price surfaced, the dossier cites it directly and it overrides the derived figures. These ranges are rough anchors for family discussion, not appraisals, and they never mix into the site's headline totals, which count only county assessed values and true model estimates.

Reproducibility

The full pipeline (county API pulls, tax lookups, entity checks, deed exports, court sweeps, weekly reconciliation) is scripted and versioned in a private repository with the complete evidence index. This site is a faithful rendering of that file, minus private-individual details excluded by design.

About this site. Every fact here comes from a public government record: county assessment and deed data, the PA Department of State, county Tax Claim lookups, and the PA court portal, each captured as a saved evidence artifact (268 entries). Market values and rents are model estimates, not appraisals. Data as of 2026-07-07; ownership is re-verified against the county weekly. Where a fact could not be verified it is marked Unverified rather than guessed.

Privacy. Court information appears only as aggregates: no tenant is named anywhere on this site, by design. Owners and deed parties are named exactly as they appear in public county records.
