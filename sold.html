The 17 the list called “sold”

The most consequential question in the whole investigation: when properties leave a family portfolio, were they really sold, by whom, to whom, for how much, and did the money leave a paper trail? We pulled the full recorded deed chain for every one of the 17 properties on the 2018 list that no longer shows a family owner. The answers sort into four buckets:

  • Two family sales in the 2020s: $122,300 in 2020 and $210,000 in 2025, both properly recorded.
  • Nine family exits from 2005 to 2016, old documented sales that predate the current questions.
  • Four never family-owned at all. The anonymous list was simply wrong about them.
  • One foreclosure (2018) and one loan: a title-holder lost 728 Olive St to a bank, and on another property the family was the lender, not the owner.

No unexplained disposition remains. Every red flag raised by a cheap-looking deed (the $1 transfers) resolved to either a family housekeeping transfer or an unrelated third-party deed.

About this site. Every fact here comes from a public government record: county assessment and deed data, the PA Department of State, county Tax Claim lookups, and the PA court portal, each captured as a saved evidence artifact (268 entries). Market values and rents are model estimates, not appraisals. Data as of 2026-07-07; ownership is re-verified against the county weekly. Where a fact could not be verified it is marked Unverified rather than guessed.

Privacy. Court information appears only as aggregates: no tenant is named anywhere on this site, by design. Owners and deed parties are named exactly as they appear in public county records.
