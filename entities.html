Who holds title, and whether the names are real

A rental portfolio holds up on paper only if its entities are registered and its owners disclosed. Here the county record is a patchwork: one real LLC holds a quarter of the parcels, a deceased individual holds another fifth, and several names are registered nowhere.

Annual report never filed; DOS says “Over Due”

Why this matters: Pennsylvania's annual-report regime (effective 2025) requires every LLC to file by September 30 each year; persistent non-filers face administrative dissolution starting 2027. None of the three has ever filed, which also means no public record names a single member or manager of any of them. Who owns the company that owns 27 houses is, on paper, unknown. Whether and who files is the family's and counsel's decision.

The names that legally exist nowhere

Five more names hold title to roughly 45 parcels (~$2.0M assessed) yet appear in no Pennsylvania registry and no fictitious-name filing: ATL Associates (22 parcels; the sole similarly named PA registrant is an unrelated Philadelphia corporation with different officers and address), Nagpaul Associates (12, all vacant land), RST Partners / RST Associates / RSP (9), Maple Ventures (1) and Galway Associates (1). They are most likely unregistered general partnerships, a form that legally exists without registration but whose partners can only be identified from private papers, original purchase deeds, or the estate. For any future sale, refinance, or insurance claim, each of these titles is a conveyance puzzle: who is authorized to sign?

Individually titled parcels round out the picture: Tony Lal (9), Alexis Hales, whose tax bills go c/o Tony Lal (3 parcels), and one each for Arvind Lal, Roby Lal, an ambiguous “LAL A”, and an Arya co-ownership.

The estate at the center

22 parcels ($1,255,810 assessed) remain titled to Amrit Lal, the founder, who has died. The recorder's index shows no executor deed, no estate document, and zero transfers out of his name. The estate has simply never been opened. Until it is, no one has clear authority over those parcels, and if he held interests in the LLCs or partnerships, those interests are frozen with it. This is the single highest-leverage item on the next-steps list.
